What is a cron expression?

A cron expression is a string of 5 or 6 fields that represents a schedule. Fields go left to right: minute, hour, day of month, month, day of week. The star (*) means every. For example, 0 9 * * 1-5 means 9:00 AM on weekdays.

What is the 6th field?

The optional 6th field is seconds. Most systems use 5-field cron (minute through weekday). Some implementations like Quartz and systemd support a leading seconds field.

How do I run something every 5 minutes?

Use */5 in the minute field: */5 * * * *. The slash means start at 0, then every 5 minutes. You can also write 0,5,10,15,20,25,30,35,40,45,50,55 for the same result.
